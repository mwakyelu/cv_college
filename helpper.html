HELPPER Program

Our Vision

To become the superior and dependable provider of solutions to accurate and affordable high-quality healthcare services to marginalized communities within their localities.

Mission Statement

To leverage healthcare provision system by deploying general and specialist medical care providers to do early detection, timely diagnosis and appropriate treatment guidance at the vicinity of the marginalized remote users

The Innovation

Invented by Dr. Salvatory Makweta Mlaga, HELPPER Program write-up has been announced as the winner by the WHO in NCD Lab First cycle 2021 (visit WHO knowledge action portal on NCD web link).

The first of its kind in our setting. Deployment by mobility, of full strategically mixed specialists catering for awareness creation, early detection > screening > pathology diagnosis and M-health tool-based referral linkages for treatment/care.

One stop center. Continuous cycles of remote areas service in time. Aligns with national and global health agenda.

Methods: HELPPER-Program

'The moving specialized hospital' is planned to run as a not for profit, comprehensive mobile healthcare polyclinics that shall travel to areas of rural and urban Tanzania to equalize access to basic health services for the underserved communities.

Scope of care

Start with on visitation site health education on NCDs, Screening, early diagnosis, treatment, and M-Health referral portal of Non-Communicable Diseases treatment linkages + Communicable diseases when clients attend.

Rural inhabitants are given an opportunity to meet medical specialists they could never meet alternatively. Initial travel system: Vehicle based polyclinics system, equipped with necessary facilities.

Special emphasis

Cancer: Breast cancer, Cancer of cervix, Prostate cancer and Childhood cancers.

Other NCDs: Diabetes Mellitus and Hypertension. Also Mental disorders, Sickle cell disease, and Malnutrition.
